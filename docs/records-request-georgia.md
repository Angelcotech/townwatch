# Georgia Open Records Act request — reusable template

Use this for any Georgia city or county records request. The legal hooks
(OCGA § 50-18-70 et seq., § 50-14-1) are Georgia-specific; for other
states create a parallel file with that state's statute references.

## Fields to fill in

| Placeholder | What it is | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `{TODAY}` | Date you're sending | `2026-05-22` |
| `{CUSTODIAN_NAME}` | Records custodian, usually City/County Clerk | `Hon. Vicki Capetillo` |
| `{CUSTODIAN_TITLE}` | Title | `City Clerk` |
| `{JURISDICTION_NAME}` | City/county legal name | `City of Grovetown` |
| `{CUSTODIAN_ADDRESS}` | Mailing address | `103 Old Wrightsboro Road, Grovetown, GA 30813` |
| `{CUSTODIAN_EMAIL}` | If you'd rather email | `clerk@cityofgrovetown.com` |
| `{GOVERNING_BODY}` | The body whose records you want | `Planning Commission` |
| `{RECORD_TYPE}` | What records | `meeting minutes` |
| `{DATE_RANGE}` | Period covered | `January 1, 2023 through the present` |
| `{SENDER_NAME}` | You | `David Brown` |
| `{SENDER_ADDRESS}` | Mailing or PO | `…` |
| `{SENDER_EMAIL}` | Reply email | `…` |
| `{SENDER_PHONE}` | Optional | `…` |

## Legal hooks (Georgia)

- **OCGA § 50-18-71(b)(1)(A)** — agency must respond within 3 business days.
- **OCGA § 50-18-71(d)** — fees: $0.10/page for copies; no fee for the first 15 minutes of search/redaction time.
- **OCGA § 50-18-71(b)(1)(B)** — if records don't exist OR aren't in custody, agency must state that in writing within 3 business days.
- **OCGA § 50-14-1(e)(2)** — minutes of all meetings must be available within "a reasonable period of time" after each meeting.
- **OCGA § 50-14-1(e)(1)** — a written summary of subjects acted upon must be available within two business days.

If the agency cites an exemption, it must point to the specific OCGA section. Common exemptions (attorney-client privilege, personnel records, security plans) generally do NOT apply to routine meeting minutes.

## Template

```
{TODAY}

{CUSTODIAN_NAME}
{CUSTODIAN_TITLE}
{JURISDICTION_NAME}
{CUSTODIAN_ADDRESS}

Sent via: email to {CUSTODIAN_EMAIL} and U.S. Mail.

RE: Georgia Open Records Act request — {GOVERNING_BODY} {RECORD_TYPE}

Dear {CUSTODIAN_NAME},

Pursuant to the Georgia Open Records Act, OCGA § 50-18-70 et seq., I
request access to and copies of the following records:

  1. All {RECORD_TYPE} of the {JURISDICTION_NAME} {GOVERNING_BODY} for
     meetings held during the period {DATE_RANGE}.

  2. For any meeting in that period for which no written {RECORD_TYPE}
     have been prepared, a written statement to that effect identifying
     the meeting by date.

  3. If draft or unapproved {RECORD_TYPE} exist for any meeting in that
     period in lieu of final approved versions, please provide those
     drafts and identify them as such.

I prefer to receive these records in electronic format (PDF, DOCX, or
the format in which they are maintained) delivered by email to
{SENDER_EMAIL}, which avoids per-page copying fees under
OCGA § 50-18-71(d).

If your agency anticipates fees exceeding $25.00 under OCGA § 50-18-71(d),
please provide a written cost estimate before incurring the cost. I will
respond to the estimate within three business days.

Under OCGA § 50-18-71(b)(1)(A), please respond to this request within
three (3) business days. If any portion of the request requires more
time to fulfill, please advise in writing within that period of (a) the
specific records that will require additional time, (b) the reason for
the delay, and (c) when the records will be produced. Under OCGA
§ 50-18-71(b)(1)(B), if any responsive records do not exist or are not
in your custody, please state that in writing within the same three
business days.

If you withhold any responsive record in whole or in part, please cite
the specific OCGA section authorizing the withholding and identify the
record being withheld with enough specificity that I can evaluate the
exemption.

Thank you for your assistance. Please reply to:

  {SENDER_NAME}
  {SENDER_ADDRESS}
  {SENDER_EMAIL}
  {SENDER_PHONE}

Sincerely,

{SENDER_NAME}
```

## Notes for reuse

- **Body types**: drop in `Board of Zoning Appeals`, `City Council`, `Downtown Development Authority`, etc.
- **Record types**: minutes, agendas, contracts, campaign finance filings, building permits, code enforcement notices — all are public records under the Act unless a specific exemption applies.
- **Date ranges**: phrase as plain English (`January 1, 2023 through the present`) rather than `2023-01-01/2026-05-22` so it scans well to clerks.
- **Across jurisdictions**: counties use Clerks of Court for some records, Clerks of Commission for others. The template wording handles either.
- **Outside Georgia**: every state's Open Records Act has analogous deadline/fee/no-records-statement provisions but the section numbers differ. Don't reuse this file verbatim for a Florida or Tennessee city.
- **Email vs hand-delivery**: Georgia accepts either. Email creates a clean paper trail (timestamp + delivery receipt). Hand-delivery at the clerk's counter creates witnessed receipt — sometimes useful if you expect resistance.
